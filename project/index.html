🏗️ The Course Project

One individual project runs the rest of the course: introduced in Class 3 (nothing due), with the first assignment — Milestone 1 — due Class 4, something due at every class through 11 (milestones at Classes 4 · 7 · 9 · 11), and a live demo on Class 12, which is presentation only. Checkpoints follow the Mon/Tue/Thu calendar: weekend-sized work lands on Mondays, Tuesday items stay light. All three tracks cover the same eight course objectives and carry the same points — choose the one that fits you.

☁️ Pocket Cloud

Turn your own laptop into a working miniature cloud provider hosting two tenant storefronts — self-service provisioning, load balancing, SSO with MFA, object storage, backups, a disaster drill, monitoring, an API split, and usage-based billing.

Runs on: your machine (Docker) · Cost: free, no credit card · Good fit if: you want everything under your fingers, offline-friendly

Project brief → Setup guide

🌊 Pelican Tours

Deploy the same booking app to real AWS three different ways — EC2 rehost, managed replatform (S3, lifecycle tiers, snapshots), then a serverless refactor (API Gateway + Lambda + DynamoDB) — with a cross-region disaster drill and a real cost model.

Runs on: AWS Academy Learner Lab · Cost: $100 class credit, no credit card · Good fit if: you want real-provider experience on your résumé

Project brief → Setup guide

📱 Bring Your Own App

Already built a mobile app or a website with an app? Run every milestone on your own system: deploy its backend, protect its admin surfaces, move its assets to object storage, harden it, survive a disaster drill, and document its API.

Runs on: your choice of the two platforms · Requires: instructor approval before Class 4 · Good fit if: you have a real app you want to level up
