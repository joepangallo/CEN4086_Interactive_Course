You are the cloud consultant. Four weeks, four deliverables, one client who never wants to buy a server again.

The client brief

Harbor Lane Books is a beloved 12-store bookstore chain across Florida's Gulf Coast: 240 employees, ~$18M annual revenue, and a loyal events program that brings bestselling authors to its flagship Sarasota store every month. The founder's daughter, Maya Delgado, just took over as CEO. Her first week included a server outage that killed online sales for 9 hours — during a signed-edition launch that had 20,000 fans refreshing the page.

Everything runs from a converted supply closet at the flagship store, maintained by a two-person IT team: Ray (25 years with the company, deeply skeptical of "renting computers from strangers") and Tasha (hired last year, quietly updating her résumé every time the AC in the closet fails). There is no disaster recovery plan. Hurricane season is four months away.

Maya's mandate to you: "Get us to the cloud safely. I want the site to survive our own success, I want my staff working from anywhere, I want AI helping customers find their next favorite book — and I never want to buy another server. Show me the plan, the costs, and the risks. Convince Ray."

Ground rules

  • Individual project. You own every deliverable end-to-end, all four weeks, and you present solo in Session 12. Discussing ideas with classmates is encouraged; the analysis, writing, and diagrams must be yours.
  • Free tools only — Google Docs/Sheets/Slides (deliverables), diagrams.net (architecture diagrams), GitHub (optional artifact hosting), free-tier AI chatbots. If a step ever asks for a credit card, stop: you've wandered off the assignment.
  • Use the course vocabulary. Each deliverable must correctly use at least three named concepts from that week's session pages (e.g., “rapid elasticity,” “warm standby,” “loose coupling”) — the session pages are your complete reference.
  • AI is your junior analyst, not your ghostwriter. You may use free AI chatbots to brainstorm, draft, and explain. Undisclosed AI use is an academic-integrity violation per the syllabus.
  • Recommendations need reasons. "Use AWS" earns nothing; "object storage for the photo archive because it's accessed rarely and scales cheaply (Session 5's storage tiers)" earns full marks. Provider-neutral answers are welcome.
