Lab 1 · SaaS Spotter

CLASSIFY · 10 MIN

Use the operational test: finished application, internet delivery, provider-managed stack, subscription/account access.

Case A

240 employees use browser email, shared calendars, and cloud documents billed monthly per user.

Case B

A public marketing page lists store hours and has no login, data, workflow, or subscription.

Case C

A sales team logs into a CRM, tracks leads, and pays per salesperson each month.

Case D

A team rents Linux VMs and installs its own database, web server, app, backups, and patches.

Case E

Support staff use a hosted AI chat assistant through a web app with company accounts.

Case F

Developers push their own checkout code; a platform builds, deploys, and scales it.

IMPLEMENT · 10 MIN

Put the rollout steps in the order you would actually run them for Harbor Lane. Use 1-5 once each.

Define owners, success measures, and exit requirements
Agree what good looks like before the vendor demo takes over.

Lab 9 · Mini Deliverable

SUBMITTABLE DRAFT · 15 MIN

Draft the SaaS part of Harbor Lane's Week 1 recommendation. Keep it concise.

Act as a skeptical cloud procurement reviewer. Review my SaaS recommendation for:
1. whether I clearly define why the solution is SaaS,
2. whether I identify what the provider manages and what the customer still owns,
3. whether I include cost, data/export, customization, internet dependency, and lock-in risks,
4. whether my recommendation is specific enough to defend.
Ask me up to five tough follow-up questions. Do not rewrite it for me.
Before leaving: your final draft should prove you can define SaaS, explain multi-tenancy, split responsibilities, model subscription cost, name risks, and plan an exit.
